Most organisations confuse issues management with crisis communications. They are related disciplines, but they are not the same. Treating them as interchangeable is one of the most common, and costly, mistakes in corporate communications.

## The distinction

**Issues management** is proactive. It is the ongoing practice of identifying, monitoring, and strategically managing emerging concerns before they escalate.

**Crisis communications** is reactive. It is what happens when an issue has escalated beyond the point of proactive management, or when an unexpected event creates immediate reputational risk.

The relationship between them is sequential: poor issues management creates crises. Good issues management prevents them.

## Why organisations skip issues management

Issues management requires investment in monitoring, analysis, and strategic planning when nothing appears to be wrong. It is easy to deprioritise when the board is focused on growth and there is no immediate fire to fight.

The problem is that issues do not announce themselves. By the time a story breaks or a stakeholder group organises, the window for proactive management has often closed.

## What good issues management looks like

- **Environmental scanning:** Systematic monitoring of media, social media, government activity, and sector trends
- **Issue identification and classification:** Ranking issues by likelihood and potential impact
- **Stakeholder mapping:** Understanding which groups care about which issues
- **Response frameworks:** Pre-approved messaging and decision trees for highest-probability issues
- **Executive briefing:** Keeping leadership informed with clear escalation criteria

## The 80/20 of crisis prevention

In our experience, approximately 80% of the crises organisations face were predictable. The organisations that avoid crises are not lucky. They have invested in issues management frameworks that catch signals early.
